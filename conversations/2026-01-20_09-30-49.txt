We present a predictive analytics framework for buildings that learns calibrated ordinal probability distributions over thermal sensation votes (TSV, –3…+3) and their expectancy, and couples these decision-ready probabilities to EnergyPlus via Sinergym. We design lightweight, interpretable thermostat policies that adjust dual setpoints by ±0.5–2.0 °C using either the expected TSV or a risk-gated rule based on predicted discomfort probability. Notably, the ordinal model mitigates extreme comfort errors and reduces directional mistakes during discomfort periods, avoiding cumulative energy penalties.
